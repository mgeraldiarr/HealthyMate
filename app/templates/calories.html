Daily Calorie Requirement Calculation Method

This calculator uses the proven accurate Mifflin St. Jeor formula to estimate your daily calorie needs.

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

BMR is the amount of calories the body needs to perform basic functions such as breathing and metabolism at rest.

Mifflin St. Jeor formula: Man: BMR = 10 x BB + 6.25 x TB - 5 x U + 5 Women: BMR = 10 x BB + 6.25 x TB - 5 x U - 161
  • BB: Body weight (kg)
  • TB: Height (cm)
  • U: Age (years)

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE)

TDEE is your total daily calorie requirement based on your physical activity level.

TDEE multiplier factor:
  • Sedentary: Little or no exercise (office job): BMR x 1.2
  • Lightly Active: Light exercise (exercise 1-2 days/week): BMR x 1.375
  • Moderately Active: Moderate exercise (exercise 3-5 days/mweek): BMR x 1.55
  • Very Active: Hard exercise (exercise 6-7 days/week): BMR x 1.725
  • Extremely Active: Very hard exercise (exercise 2x days/more): BMR x 1.9

Knowing your TDEE can help you manage your diet and activity according to your health goals.
